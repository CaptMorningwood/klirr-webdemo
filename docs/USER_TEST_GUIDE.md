# Klirr User Test Guide

## Goal
Evaluate whether a new user understands:

- what Klirr is
- what their life costs every month
- what a sustainable Budget means
- what Budget Health means
- how to use Budget Buddy safely

The test should show whether Klirr feels coherent, trustworthy, mobile-friendly, and focused on recurring income, recurring costs, Rörlig Budget, margin, and a sustainable Budget.

## Recommended participants
Run five moderated usability tests. Prefer a mix of:

- people with low technical confidence
- people with no budgeting experience
- people with some budgeting experience
- mobile-first users
- people outside the product team’s social circle if possible

Avoid explaining Klirr in advance beyond: “This is a product that helps you understand and plan your recurring Budget.”

## Test tasks
Ask the participant to think aloud while doing these tasks:

1. Try to understand what your life costs every month.
2. Add or import income and recurring costs.
3. Create a sustainable Budget.
4. Change something using Budget Buddy.
5. Run Budget Checkup.
6. Explain Budget Health in your own words.
7. Find and edit a recurring must.
8. Explain what happens before confirming a Buddy action.

## Moderator rules

- do not explain too early
- do not lead the participant
- ask “What do you think will happen if you press that?”
- ask “What are you looking for now?”
- note hesitation
- note misunderstood words
- note trust concerns
- note positive emotional reactions

If the participant gets stuck, wait a few seconds before helping. When help is needed, give the smallest hint possible and mark the task as not completed without help.

## Post-test questions

- What do you think Klirr is for?
- What does Budget Health mean?
- What felt most useful?
- What felt unclear?
- What felt unsafe?
- Would you use a real bank statement?
- What would make you return?
- What would make you pay for Klirr?

## Severity scale

- **Critical:** blocks completion or creates dangerous misunderstanding
- **High:** major confusion or trust loss
- **Medium:** friction but task can be completed
- **Low:** cosmetic or small wording issue

### Recommended next sprint
List the smallest set of fixes that would most improve understanding, trust, and mobile completion. Keep recommendations aligned with Budget First principles.

### Features explicitly not requested by users
Track requests that did **not** appear, especially if the team expected them. This helps protect Klirr from drifting into a bank, bookkeeping app, transaction diary, or historical month-comparison product.
